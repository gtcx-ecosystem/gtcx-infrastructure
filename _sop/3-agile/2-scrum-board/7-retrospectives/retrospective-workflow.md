# Retrospective Workflow

How to run sprint retrospectives effectively — from preparation through follow-up.

---

## Schedule

| Type                    | Frequency                | Duration  | Participants             |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------ | --------- | ------------------------ |
| Sprint retrospective    | Every sprint (end)       | 1–2 hours | Full team + Scrum Master |
| Release retrospective   | After major releases     | 2–3 hours | Full team + stakeholders |
| Quarterly retrospective | Every quarter            | 2–4 hours | Full team                |
| Incident retrospective  | After significant issues | 1–2 hours | Affected team members    |

Timing: after sprint review, before next sprint planning.

---

## Process Flow

### Phase 1 — Preparation (15 min)

1. Gather sprint metrics (velocity, completion rate, defect count)
2. Set up collaboration space (board, doc, or in-person wall)
3. Distribute agenda to participants
4. Establish ground rules: focus on process not people, psychological safety

### Phase 2 — Data Gathering (20 min)

1. Review sprint metrics as a group
2. Collect individual observations (What went well? What didn't? Blockers? Surprises?)
3. Cluster similar observations

### Phase 3 — Insights (25 min)

1. Identify recurring patterns
2. Root cause analysis on key issues (use 5 Whys)
3. Prioritize by impact (dot voting or group consensus)

### Phase 4 — Action Planning (20 min)

1. Define specific, actionable improvements
2. Assign P0/P1/P2 priorities
3. Assign owners with clear accountability
4. Set target sprints for completion

### Phase 5 — Integration and Closure (10 min)

1. Decide which actions go into next sprint vs. backlog
2. Review previous sprint's retrospective actions
3. Confirm everyone understands and commits to their items

## Facilitation Techniques

### Data gathering

- **Start/Stop/Continue** — three-column gather
- **4Ls** — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for
- **Mad/Sad/Glad** — emotional response mapping
- **Sailboat** — what propelled vs. anchored the team

### Root cause analysis

- **5 Whys** — drill down on a single issue
- **Fishbone diagram** — categorize causes systematically
- **Affinity mapping** — group related themes

### Prioritization

- **Dot voting** — each person gets 3–5 dots to allocate
- **Impact vs. Effort matrix** — quick wins vs. big bets

## Action Item Triage

After every retrospective, categorize actions:

| Category  | Criteria                                     | Destination                 |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Immediate | High impact, low effort — do next sprint     | Sprint backlog              |
| Planned   | High impact, high effort — requires planning | Backlog item (prioritized)  |
| Deferred  | Low impact — nice to have                    | Backlog item (low priority) |
| Dropped   | Low impact, high effort                      | Explicit decision to park   |

---

## Tracking

- **Action items:** logged in retrospective doc with owner, target sprint, success criteria
- **Review cadence:** previous actions reviewed at the start of every retrospective
- **Metrics to track:**
  - Action item completion rate
  - Sprint velocity trend
  - Defect trend
  - Team satisfaction scores

---

## Documentation

Required outputs per retrospective:

- Retrospective summary (use `retrospective.md` template)
- Action items (owners, target sprints, success criteria)
- Process changes (any immediate adjustments to workflow)

File naming: `sprint-{N}-retro.md`

---

## Facilitation Best Practices

- Keep it timeboxed — strictly 15 min per phase
- Rotate facilitators to share the skill and avoid bias
- Focus on systems and processes, not individuals
- Celebrate successes before diving into problems
- End with genuine team commitment — not passive agreement
- Follow up on actions at next standup, not just next retro
