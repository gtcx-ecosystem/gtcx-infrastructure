# Risk Register

> Product, technical, business, and editorial risks for [Organization Name].

---

## Product Risks

### Risk: Content quality insufficient for institutional audience

- **Impact:** High — credibility is the core asset
- **Mitigation:** Editorial standards with quality scoring (minimum [X]–[X] by product), human editorial gates on all content, source hierarchy enforcement (Tier 1–4), correction policy
- **Owner:** Editorial team

### Risk: [Primary Index] methodology challenged or not adopted

- **Impact:** High — indices are the quantitative backbone
- **Mitigation:** Transparent methodology (publicly auditable), peer review process, stakeholder validation (government, industry, civil society), [X]+ independent data sources per score
- **Owner:** Index team

### Risk: Agentic production creates errors at scale

- **Impact:** Medium — factual errors damage credibility
- **Mitigation:** Human gate on all published content, QA validation against quality standards, breaking alert protocol requires editor approval, accuracy targets: [X]%+ for [high-accuracy products]
- **Owner:** Engineering + Editorial

### Risk: Partner or government relationships slow to develop

- **Impact:** Medium — significant revenue depends on institutional partnerships
- **Mitigation:** Start with open-access policy briefs to build trust, leverage [Index] scores as engagement tool, target institutions with faster procurement cycles first, advisory projects as relationship builders
- **Owner:** Business development

## Technical Risks

### Risk: Data source access unreliable ([Intelligence Service])

- **Impact:** Medium — monitoring depends on third-party source availability
- **Mitigation:** [Digital Infrastructure Index] scores drive monitoring approach (more manual for low-scoring jurisdictions), multiple source types per market, >[X]% capture rate target, multi-language extraction capability
- **Owner:** Data engineering

### Risk: Platform scalability under load

- **Impact:** Low (initially) — small user base at launch
- **Mitigation:** Cloud autoscaling, caching layer, CDN for static content, [X]% uptime target, monitoring with [X]-minute response SLA
- **Owner:** Infrastructure team

## Business Risks

### Risk: Subscriber conversion too slow

- **Impact:** High — revenue depends on paid conversions
- **Mitigation:** Founding member pricing, target LTV:CAC ratio provides margin for aggressive acquisition, free tier builds habit before conversion, content quality creates lock-in
- **Owner:** Growth team

### Risk: Brand confusion between [Organization Name] and partner protocols / platforms

- **Impact:** Medium — unclear positioning weakens both brands
- **Mitigation:** Clear entity split documented, [Organization Name] as distinct product brand, separate websites and materials, consistent messaging guidelines
- **Owner:** Marketing

### Risk: Competitor enters the [category] space

- **Impact:** Medium — first-mover advantage is temporary
- **Mitigation:** [Partner Protocol] integration creates ground-truth data moat, [Registry Platform] creates network effects, [Primary Index] becomes cited standard (hard to displace), deep market expertise not easily replicated
- **Owner:** Strategy

---

## Editorial Risks

### Risk: Editorial independence compromised by commercial pressure

- **Impact:** Critical — credibility is existential
- **Mitigation:** Organizational firewall, Editorial Review Board, conflict of interest disclosure + annual certification, whistleblower protection, no pre-publication review by subjects
- **Owner:** Editor-in-chief

### Risk: Source or subject safety ([Field Publication])

- **Impact:** Critical — human safety is non-negotiable
- **Mitigation:** Safety protocol before every field operation, risk assessment for correspondents and subjects, anonymization when required, no story worth endangering anyone, appropriate insurance coverage
- **Owner:** [Field Publication] editor

### Risk: Diplomatic or reputational incident from [Market Corridor] content

- **Impact:** High — institutional relationships are strategic
- **Mitigation:** Diplomatic language review, government relations sign-off process, options framework (present alternatives, not recommendations), sovereignty respected in all framing
- **Owner:** [Market] team + government relations
