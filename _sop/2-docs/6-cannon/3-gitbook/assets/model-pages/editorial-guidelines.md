## The [Organization Name] Voice

### Who We Are

We are the intelligence arm of the verification economy. Our readers are smart, busy professionals who need accurate information to make consequential decisions. They don't have time for fluff, but they value context and nuance.

### Voice Principles

| Principle          | What It Means                              | Example                                                                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Authoritative**  | We know this space deeply                  | "[Authority] has..." not "It appears that [Authority] may have..."                    |
| **Accessible**     | Complex ideas, clear language              | "The new rule requires exporters to..." not "Pursuant to regulatory modifications..." |
| **Actionable**     | Every piece should inform a decision       | Include "what this means for you"                                                     |
| **[Market]-first** | Your market is the subject, not the exotic | "[Country] announced" not "The [adjective] nation of [Country] announced"             |
| **Honest**         | Acknowledge uncertainty                    | "Details remain unclear" when they do                                                 |

### What We Sound Like

**We sound like:**

- A knowledgeable colleague briefing you before a meeting
- The analyst who's done the work so you don't have to
- A trusted advisor who respects your time

**We don't sound like:**

- Academic papers (too dense)
- Press releases (too promotional)
- Consultants (too vague)
- AI-generated content (too generic)

## Writing Standards

### Headlines

Headlines should be specific, informative, and searchable.

| ❌ Don't                             | ✅ Do                                                   |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Big Changes in [Country]"           | "[Country] Mandates [Requirement] for [Domain] Exports" |
| "What You Need to Know About [Term]" | "[Term]: [Year] Policy Tracker for [X] Countries"       |
| "Important Update"                   | "[Authority] Updates [Topic] Guidance: [X] Key Changes" |

**Formula:** [Actor] + [Action] + [Specificity]

### Leads

The first sentence should answer: What happened? Why does it matter?

**Example:**

> ✅ "[Country]'s [Authority] will require [requirement] for all [domain] exports beginning [date], a move that affects an estimated [X]+ [entities] and their [counterparties]."

> ❌ "In a significant development for the [sector], authorities in [Country] have announced important changes that will impact the industry."

### Structure

**For News ([Platform D]):**

1. **Lead** — What happened, why it matters
2. **Details** — Key facts, numbers, quotes
3. **Context** — Background, how this fits the bigger picture
4. **What's Next** — Timeline, expected impacts
5. **Related** — Links to relevant content

**For Analysis ([Platform C]):**

1. **Executive Summary** — Key findings and implications
2. **Background** — Context and history
3. **Analysis** — Data, evidence, interpretation
4. **Implications** — What this means for different stakeholders
5. **Methodology** — How we reached these conclusions

**For Briefs ([Platform C]):**

1. **Bottom Line** — One sentence summary
2. **What Happened** — The facts
3. **Why It Matters** — Impact assessment
4. **What to Do** — Recommended actions
5. **Sources** — Where to learn more

## Style Guide

### Geography

| Rule                                                         | Example                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Use country names, not demonyms in leads                     | "[Country] announced" not "[Demonym] officials announced" |
| Specify regions on first reference                           | "[Region]'s leading [industry] producer, [Country]"       |
| Don't default to the region when you mean a specific country | If it's [Country], say [Country]                          |

### Numbers & Data

| Type          | Format                                            |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Currency      | $1.2 billion, $450 million, $25,000               |
| Percentages   | 15%, not 15 percent (except in formal reports)    |
| Large numbers | 1.2 million, not 1,200,000                        |
| Dates         | January 28, 2026 or Jan 28, 2026 (not 28/01/2026) |
| Time          | 14:00 UTC (always specify timezone)               |

### Terminology

| Use                       | Instead Of            | Why                                  |
| ------------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| [Domain term — preferred] | [Domain term — avoid] | [Reason for preference]              |
| Verification              | Certification         | Our specific value proposition       |
| Compliance score          | Rating                | Avoids confusion with credit ratings |
| [Domain term — preferred] | [Domain term — avoid] | [Reason for preference]              |
| Due diligence             | Compliance checks     | Industry standard term               |

### Abbreviations

Spell out on first use, then abbreviate:

> "The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) released updated guidance. The OECD's new framework..."

**Exception:** Well-known abbreviations that readers in your domain know (e.g., USD, EU, UN, [domain-specific abbreviation]).

## Content Types

### 🚨 Breaking Alert ([Platform D])

**When to publish:** Within 4 hours of significant regulatory or market development.

**Format:**

- 100-200 words
- One key takeaway
- Link to source
- No analysis (save for follow-up)

**Example:**

```
🚨 BREAKING: [Country/Entity] [Announces/Mandates] [Policy/Development]

[Country/Entity] [announced/released/mandated] today that [summary of change]
beginning [date].

KEY DETAILS:
• Applies to [scope]
• [Key requirement or condition]
• [Timeline or transition period]

The move affects an estimated [X]+ [entities] and positions
[Country/Entity] as the [first/leading] [jurisdiction/organization] to [milestone].

Source: [Official source name and type]
[Full statement →]

We'll publish analysis in [tomorrow's / the next] [publication name].
```

**When to publish:** Quarterly or for major developments.

**Format:**

- Original research and analysis

- Minimum 10 primary sources
- Expert review before publication
- 48-hour fact-check window
- Explicit uncertainty acknowledgment

## Sourcing Standards

### Source Hierarchy

| Tier | Source Type                                        | Usage                                    |
| :--: | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
|  1   | Official government documents, verified statements | Always cite                              |
|  2   | Named industry sources, public company filings     | Prefer                                   |
|  3   | Anonymous industry sources (verified)              | Use sparingly, explain why anonymous     |
|  4   | Secondary media reports                            | Only if primary unavailable, always link |

### Attribution

**Government sources:**

> "According to [Country]'s [Authority]..."
> "The Ministry of Mines stated..."
> "Per the official gazette..."

**Industry sources (named):**

> "[Name], [Title] at [Organization], told [Organization Name]..."

**Industry sources (anonymous):**

> "According to a senior compliance officer at a major European refiner who spoke on condition of anonymity due to ongoing negotiations..."

**Never:**

- "Sources say" (who?)
- "It is believed that" (by whom?)
- "Industry experts" (which ones?)

### Verification

Before publishing:

- [ ] Primary source verified (not just wire service report)
- [ ] Numbers independently confirmed where possible
- [ ] Context accurate (are we misrepresenting significance?)
- [ ] Stakeholder impact correctly characterized
- [ ] Dissenting views acknowledged if significant

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content:

### Accuracy

- [ ] Facts verified against primary sources
- [ ] Numbers checked and formatted correctly
- [ ] Names and titles confirmed
- [ ] Dates and timelines accurate

### Clarity

- [ ] Lead answers what/why in first sentence
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Acronyms spelled out on first use
- [ ] Structure matches content type template

### Value

- [ ] Reader learns something they didn't know
- [ ] Implications for decision-making clear
- [ ] Context sufficient for new readers
- [ ] Links to relevant background included

### Voice

- [ ] Authoritative but not arrogant
- [ ] [Market]-first framing (your market is the subject, not the exotic)
- [ ] Appropriate for platform and audience
- [ ] Honest about uncertainty

- **[Protocol Partner] development** — We cover protocol news objectively
