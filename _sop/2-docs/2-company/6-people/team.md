## Hiring Principles

### What We Look For

1. **Domain expertise** — Deep knowledge of [core market/domain]
2. **Market experience** — Has operated in the environments we serve
3. **Intellectual honesty** — Clear about what they know and don't know
4. **Mission alignment** — Believes the infrastructure we're building matters
5. **Operating in ambiguity** — Comfortable building without playbooks

### What We Avoid

1. **Consultants who've never built** — Advice without execution
2. **Big company operators** — Need process and hierarchy to function
3. **Mission tourists** — Mission without capability
4. **Equity chasers** — Compensation without commitment

## Culture Principles

### 1. One Team

No "headquarters" and "field." No "product" and "operations." One mission, one team.

### 2. Default to Written

Important decisions get documented. Work is visible. Asynchronous by default.

### 3. Intellectual Honesty

Say what you know and what you don't. Uncertainty is stated. Errors are acknowledged.

### 4. Constraints First

If it works under the most constrained conditions, it works everywhere.

### 5. Elegant Efficiency

Small team, big impact. Lean operations. Technology that multiplies output.

_The team is the strategy. Hire well, then get out of the way._
