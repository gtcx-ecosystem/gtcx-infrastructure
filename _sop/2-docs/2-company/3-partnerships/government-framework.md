## Partnership Tiers

### Tier 1: Data Partnership (No Cost)

**Commitment**: Provide data feeds, validate content
**Benefit**: [Index Name] coverage, recognition in methodology

Appropriate for:

- Jurisdictions in early engagement
- Ministries building relationship
- Testing value before deeper commitment

### Tier 2: Strategic Partnership

**Commitment**: Active collaboration, expert access, co-marketing
**Benefit**: Priority coverage, custom analysis, advisory pricing

Deliverables:

- Quarterly briefings to ministry
- Custom [Index Name] deep dive report
- Speaking opportunities at [Organization Name] events
- [N]% discount on advisory services

### Tier 3: Anchor Partnership

**Commitment**: Multi-year MOU, data integration, joint programs
**Benefit**: Full advisory access, policy co-development, investment attraction support

Deliverables:

- Monthly intelligence briefings
- Custom [platform access] for ministry staff
- "Improve Your [Index] Score" program included
- Joint investor outreach
- Annual summit participation

## Messaging Framework

### Opening Pitch

_"We're building the first comprehensive [infrastructure/compliance/intelligence] index for [sector] in [region]. Think of it as [analogy — e.g., a credit rating], but for [what it measures]. We want to ensure [Country] is accurately represented and well-positioned in global [investor/buyer/partner] perception."_

### Value Proposition

_"Our [Index Name] score will be referenced by [traders/investors/institutions]. A strong score attracts [investment/partnerships]. A clear improvement roadmap shows momentum. We want to partner with you to ensure accuracy and identify opportunities."_

### Objection Handling

**"Why should we share data with you?"**
_"Everything we publish is already public — we're just organizing it systematically. Your participation ensures accuracy and lets you shape the narrative."_

**"How is this different from existing ratings?"**
_"Existing indices are [perception surveys / policy reviews]. [Index Name] is [measurement-based / outcome-focused] — actual [infrastructure / processes / timelines]. Much harder to game, much more useful for real decisions."_

**"What if our score is low?"**
_"Then you have a roadmap for improvement. We've seen jurisdictions gain [N]+ points in [N] years with focused effort. A low score with an improvement trajectory is more attractive to [investors/partners] than a static high score."_
