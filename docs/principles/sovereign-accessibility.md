# Sovereign Accessibility Principles

**Making verification infrastructure available to everyone who needs it**

---

## Core Principle

Infrastructure should serve people, not extract from them. Sovereign accessibility means building systems that enhance local capability rather than create dependency.

---

## 15 Sub-Principles

### Access & Inclusion

**1. Offline-First Design**
Systems must work without continuous connectivity. Field conditions—not urban assumptions—drive architecture decisions.

**2. Progressive Enhancement**
Start with the simplest viable interface (SMS, USSD), enhance for better connectivity. Never require smartphones.

**3. Language Sovereignty**
Support local languages as first-class citizens, not afterthoughts. Twi, Swahili, Hausa are as important as English.

**4. Cost Transparency**
Users must understand what they're paying and why. No hidden fees, no surprise charges.

**5. Data Ownership**
Producers own their data. Platforms are custodians, not owners. Export must always be possible.

### Capability Building

**6. Training as Infrastructure**
Education is not optional—it's core infrastructure. Every verification touchpoint is a learning opportunity.

**7. Local Expertise Development**
Build local capability, not dependency. Train trainers, certify local experts, create career paths.

**8. Graduated Complexity**
Start simple, add sophistication as users grow. Don't overwhelm day-one users with enterprise features.

**9. Peer Learning Networks**
Enable users to help each other. Formal training + informal knowledge sharing.

**10. Vernacular Documentation**
Technical documentation in accessible language. Diagrams over jargon.

### Economic Justice

**11. Value Capture Locally**
Economic benefits should accrue to producing communities, not just intermediaries.

**12. Transparent Pricing**
Price discovery should benefit sellers, not just buyers. Information asymmetry is the enemy.

**13. Fair Revenue Sharing**
Platform economics must visibly benefit all participants, especially those with least power.

**14. Cooperative Models**
Support collective ownership structures. Individual success should strengthen community.

**15. Anti-Extraction Design**
Actively prevent rent-seeking behavior. Design systems that make exploitation harder.
