# Regenerative Economics Principles

> **Status:** Current
> **Date:** 2026-05-10
> **Owner:** GTCX Infrastructure

**Building systems that create more value than they extract**

---

## Core Principle

Traditional extractive models take value from commodity-producing regions. Regenerative economics inverts this—infrastructure should strengthen local economies, not drain them.

---

## 15 Sub-Principles

### Value Creation

**1. Additive, Not Extractive**
Every platform interaction should leave participants better off. Zero-sum thinking is design failure.

**2. Compound Benefits**
Design for benefits that grow over time. Reputation, relationships, and capability compound.

**3. Ecosystem Thinking**
Individual transactions exist within systems. Optimize for ecosystem health, not transaction volume.

**4. Long-Term Alignment**
Incentive structures should favor long-term relationship building over short-term extraction.

**5. Shared Prosperity**
Platform success should visibly correlate with participant success. If we win while they lose, we've failed.

### Market Design

**6. Price Discovery Justice**
Information infrastructure should help price-takers become price-makers. Transparency benefits the informed.

**7. Counterparty Quality**
Better information about counterparties reduces risk and builds trust. Verification creates value.

**8. Transaction Cost Reduction**
Lower friction benefits everyone. Reduce paperwork, simplify processes, eliminate unnecessary intermediaries.

**9. Risk Distribution**
Spread risk appropriately across parties who can bear it. Don't concentrate risk on the vulnerable.

**10. Market Access Expansion**
Every verified producer gains market access. Formalization opens doors, not closes them.

### Community Wealth

**11. Local Multipliers**
Platform activity should generate local employment, local expertise, local enterprise.

**12. Knowledge Commons**
Industry intelligence benefits everyone. Open data where possible, proprietary only where necessary.

**13. Infrastructure Investment**
Platform revenues should fund infrastructure that benefits all participants.

**14. Cooperative Capital**
Support financial structures that keep capital circulating locally.

**15. Intergenerational Thinking**
Build for the next generation, not just the next quarter. Sustainable beats fast.
