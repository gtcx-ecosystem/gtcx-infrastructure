# Alternative Network Concepts

**Strategic Exploration: Pre-[Platform A]/[Platform B] Naming**

---

## Overview

Before settling on [Platform A]/[Platform B], three parallel network concepts were explored. This document captures these for reference, as some elements may be useful for future brand extensions or positioning.

---

## Concept 1: The [Platform B]

_"[Market] intelligence for people who move markets."_

### Positioning

Premium intelligence publication + community for professionals who need to know what's actually happening in [target market] — investors, analysts, journalists, corporate strategists, operators.

### The Information Model Applied

| Element           | The Information                                   | The [Platform B]                                 |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Core product**  | Tech news for executives                          | [Market] intelligence for market-movers          |
| **Premium price** | $399-999/year                                     | $[X]-$[X]/year                                   |
| **Community**     | Subscriber Slack, directory, events               | Subscriber community, source network, convenings |
| **Positioning**   | "Silicon Valley's most-read executive newsletter" | "[Market]'s most trusted intelligence source"    |
| **Status signal** | "I read The Information"                          | "I subscribe to The [Platform B]"                |

### Product Tiers

| Tier                       | Price        | What You Get                                             |
| -------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The [Platform F] Daily** | Free         | Daily briefing, curated headlines, occasional deep dives |
| **Subscriber**             | $299/year    | Full access: all analysis, data, community, directory    |
| **Professional**           | $599/year    | + Source network access, contributor platform, events    |
| **Institutional**          | $2,500+/year | Multi-seat, custom briefings, analyst access             |

### Key Taglines

- "The narrative about [target market] is written by people who fly in for three days. The [Platform F] is written by people who live the markets."
- "If you move [resources/capital] into or out of [target market], you need The [Platform B]."
- "Everyone else is trading on yesterday's information."

### Why Not Selected

The [Platform A] positioned as general [market] intelligence — too broad. [Platform B] is narrower ([specific domain]) and deeper. The [Platform C] could exist as a separate brand but risks diluting focus.

## Concept 2: The Guild

_"The professional standard for frontier market compliance."_

### Positioning

Professional association + peer network for compliance professionals working in frontier and emerging markets. Think ACAMS meets The Information — credential, community, intelligence.

### Why "The Guild"

Guilds are ancient institutions of **craft, standards, and peer verification**. You don't just join a guild — you're admitted by peers who vouch for your work. Guild members set the standards for their trade.

### Membership Tiers

| Tier          | Who                      | Cost               | What You Get                                        |
| ------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Associate** | Early-career, candidates | $150/year          | Intelligence, tools, community, events              |
| **Member**    | 3+ years, peer-verified  | $500/year          | + Directory listing, working groups, credential     |
| **Fellow**    | 10+ years, nominated     | $1,000/year        | + "Guild Fellow" designation, leadership roles      |
| **Corporate** | Institutions             | $5,000-25,000/year | Multi-seat, recruitment access, branded partnership |

### The Credential

**Guild Member:**

- 3+ years relevant experience
- Two endorsements from existing members
- Application reviewed by Membership Committee
- Annual continuing education requirement

**Guild Fellow:**

- 10+ years experience
- Nominated by two existing Fellows
- Demonstrated contribution to the field
- Interview with Fellowship Committee

### What Members Get

**Intelligence:**

- Guild Brief — weekly regulatory intelligence across frontier markets
- Enforcement Tracker — who's getting fined, why, what it means
- Jurisdiction Guides — deep dives on compliance requirements by country

**Tools:**

- Guild Templates — standardized KYC, due diligence, SAR templates
- Risk Framework — common methodology for high-risk jurisdiction assessment

**Community:**

- Regional Chapters — [Region A], [Region B], [Region C], [Region D]
- Sector Working Groups — [Sector A], [Sector B], [Sector C], [Sector D]
- Peer Forums — small groups for confidential peer support

**Recognition:**

- Guild 50 — annual list of most influential compliance professionals
- Guild Awards — recognizing excellence at the Annual Assembly

### Why Not Selected

The Guild targets compliance professionals specifically, not [primary audience] broadly. Could be developed as separate vertical but risks fragmenting focus. Some elements (credential, peer verification) informed [Platform B] verification tiers.

## Concept 3: The Table

_"Where principals do business."_

### Positioning

Peer attestation network + deal flow platform for principals in [target domain]. No intermediaries. No noise. Just verified principals doing deals.

### Why "The Table"

"Having a seat at the table" = power, access, trust. The Table is where decisions happen. If you're at The Table, you belong.

### What It Offers

**Registry:**

- Peer attestation — principals vouch for each other
- Track record — verified history of deals closed
- Counterparty intelligence — anonymized data on payment history, delivery reliability
- Dispute resolution — mediation framework for member-to-member issues

**Deal Flow:**

- The Room — private, verified environment for principals to post and find opportunities
- [Platform A] Networks — [Region A], [Region B], [Region C], [Region D]
- Introductions — facilitated connections between verified principals

**Intelligence:**

- Table Brief — market intelligence, corridor trends, regulatory shifts
- Compliance Navigation — [Regulatory standards and certifications] decoded

**Convenings:**

- Quarterly Roundtables — [City A], [City B], [City C], [City D]
- Annual Dinner — the gathering where the year's biggest players are recognized

### Why Not Selected

"[Selected name]" tested better. "The Table" feels exclusive in a way that might alienate smaller operators. "[Selected name]" is more functional, more accessible, and the name does the work without explanation.

## Naming Principles Learned

**What worked:**

- **Short** — one or two words
- **Evocative** — conjures something tangible
- **Could have existed for 50 years** — not startup-y
- **You'd put it on LinkedIn** — status signal
- **You'd say it out loud** — sounds right in conversation

**Examples:**

- "I'm in [Platform A]" ✓
- "I subscribe to The [Platform B]" ✓
- "I'm a Guild Member" ✓
- "I have a seat at The Table" ✓

**What didn't work:**

- "The Open Compliance Council" ✗ (sounds like a committee)
- "The Principals Exchange" ✗ (generic)
- "[Platform A] Compliance Network" ✗ (corporate)
