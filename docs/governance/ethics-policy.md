# Ethics Policy

## 1. Purpose

Establish the ethical standards and conduct expectations for all GTCX directors, officers, employees, contractors, and partners. This policy supports King IV Principle 1 (Ethical leadership) and Principle 2 (Organizational ethics).

## 2. Core Values

| Value              | Definition                                         | Expected Behaviour                                                               |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Integrity**      | Honesty in all dealings                            | No falsification of records, no misleading statements to regulators or customers |
| **Accountability** | Ownership of decisions and outcomes                | Prompt reporting of errors; no blame-shifting                                    |
| **Fairness**       | Equitable treatment of all stakeholders            | No discrimination; transparent pricing; fair dispute resolution                  |
| **Transparency**   | Openness in governance and operations              | Timely disclosure of material issues to board and regulators                     |
| **Respect**        | Dignity for customers, colleagues, and communities | Privacy protection; cultural sensitivity; constructive communication             |

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal proceedings:

### 3.1 Financial Misconduct

- Fraud, embezzlement, or theft of company or customer assets.
- Bribery or corruption in any form (giving or receiving).
- Insider trading or misuse of material non-public information.
- Falsification of financial records or audit trails.

### 3.2 Conflicts of Interest

- Personal financial interest in a GTCX competitor, supplier, or customer without disclosure.
- Using GTCX assets or information for personal gain.
- Accepting gifts > $200 from partners or vendors without disclosure.
- Directors must recuse themselves from decisions where a conflict exists.

### 3.3 Data and Privacy

- Unauthorized access to customer or employee personal information.
- Selling or sharing data with third parties without contractual basis.
- Circumventing access controls or audit mechanisms.

### 3.4 Harassment and Discrimination

- Any form of harassment (sexual, racial, religious, or otherwise).
- Discrimination in hiring, promotion, or compensation.
- Retaliation against whistleblowers.

### 3.5 AI and Algorithmic Ethics

- Training AI models on data without proper consent or license.
- Manipulating model outputs to disadvantage specific demographic groups.
- Concealing known algorithmic biases from customers or regulators.
- Using AI to make consequential decisions without human oversight where required by policy.

## 4. Whistleblower Protection

| Element                | Policy                                                                     |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Reporting channels** | ethics@gtcx.io (encrypted), anonymous web form, direct board contact       |
| **Confidentiality**    | Identity protected to the maximum extent permitted by law                  |
| **Non-retaliation**    | Any retaliation against a whistleblower is grounds for immediate dismissal |
| **Investigation**      | Independent investigation by Board Security Committee or external counsel  |
| **Escalation**         | Material findings reported to Board within 48 hours                        |

## 5. Gifts and Hospitality

| Value     | Approval Required     | Disclosure Required      |
| --------- | --------------------- | ------------------------ |
| ≤ $100    | No                    | No                       |
| $100–$200 | Direct manager        | Yes (annual declaration) |
| $200–$500 | Department head       | Yes (within 7 days)      |
| > $500    | CEO + Compliance Lead | Yes (within 24 hours)    |

Cash gifts of any value are prohibited.

## 6. Political Activities

GTCX does not make political contributions. Employees may engage in personal political activity on their own time, provided they:

- Do not use GTCX assets or resources.
- Do not represent themselves as speaking for GTCX.
- Do not engage in political activity that would harm GTCX's reputation or regulatory standing.

## 8. Training and Acknowledgment

All employees must:

1. Read and acknowledge this policy within 5 days of onboarding.
2. Complete annual ethics training (online module + case study discussion).
3. Submit annual declaration of conflicts of interest and gifts received.
