# Editorial Independence Policy

> **Status:** Current
> **Date:** 2026-05-10
> **Owner:** GTCX Infrastructure

**Protecting the integrity of [Organization Name] journalism**

---

## Core Principle

[Organization Name] editorial operations are independent from commercial, investor, and partnership interests. Credibility is our most valuable asset.

---

## Independence Structure

### Organizational Firewall

```
COMMERCIAL OPERATIONS              EDITORIAL OPERATIONS
─────────────────────              ────────────────────
• Sales                            • News coverage
• Partnerships                     • Analysis
• Investor relations               • Research
• Business development             • Index methodology

         ↓ FIREWALL ↓

No commercial influence on:
• Story selection
• Coverage decisions
• Publication timing
• Index calculations
```

### Reporting Lines

The Editor-in-Chief reports to the Chief Content Officer, not the CEO or commercial leadership. This ensures editorial decisions are made on journalistic merit.

---

## Prohibited Practices

### Never Allowed

| Practice                                         | Rationale               |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------- |
| Advertiser influence on coverage                 | Compromises credibility |
| Partner approval of content                      | Creates conflicts       |
| Payment for positive coverage                    | Corruption              |
| Suppression of negative coverage                 | Deception               |
| Index methodology changes for commercial reasons | Fraud                   |
| Source access trading                            | Ethical violation       |

### Requires Disclosure

| Situation                                      | Required Action       |
| ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Coverage of [Protocol Partner] (sister entity) | Disclose relationship |
| Coverage of investors/partners                 | Disclose relationship |
| Coverage of competitors                        | No special treatment  |
| Personal investments by staff                  | Mandatory disclosure  |

---

## Conflict of Interest Policy

### Staff Requirements

1. **Disclosure:** All staff must disclose financial interests in covered sectors
2. **Trading restrictions:** No trading on non-public information
3. **Gift policy:** No gifts >$50 from sources or covered entities
4. **Outside work:** No paid work for covered entities without approval

### Enforcement

Violations result in:

- First offense: Written warning + training
- Second offense: Suspension
- Third offense: Termination

Serious violations (trading, bribery) result in immediate termination and potential legal action.

---

## Editorial Review Board

An independent Editorial Review Board meets quarterly to:

- Review editorial independence compliance
- Hear complaints about editorial decisions
- Advise on sensitive coverage questions
- Report to the Board of Directors

### Board Composition

- 1 external journalist (chair)
- 1 academic (journalism/media ethics)
- 1 industry expert (not current advertiser/partner)
- Editor-in-Chief (non-voting)

---

## Advertiser/Partner Guidelines

### What Partners Can Expect

- Professional, accurate coverage
- Fair opportunity to respond to criticism
- Clear distinction between news and sponsored content

### What Partners Cannot Expect

- Advance notice of negative coverage
- Editorial approval rights
- Suppression of unfavorable stories
- Favorable treatment in exchange for business

### Sponsored Content

All sponsored content must be:

- Clearly labeled as sponsored/promotional
- Visually distinct from editorial content
- Reviewed by editorial for factual accuracy
- Never presented as independent journalism

---

## Index Independence

[Index Name] and other indices are calculated using:

- Published methodology
- Transparent data sources
- Consistent application
- No commercial adjustments

**Index methodology changes require:**

1. Public notice (30 days minimum)
2. Methodology documentation update
3. Editorial Review Board notification
4. No commercial input in decision

---

## Whistleblower Protection

Staff who report editorial independence violations are protected from retaliation. Reports can be made to:

- Editorial Review Board (anonymous option)
- Chief Content Officer
- Board of Directors

---

_Editorial independence is non-negotiable. It is the foundation of everything we build._
