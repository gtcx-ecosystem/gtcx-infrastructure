## Product Identity

Markets OS is an AI-native capital formation and transaction operating system:

```text
originate -> verify -> configure bankability -> discover capital
-> build confidential demand -> assure and approve -> admit live
-> close in 7/14/30 days -> own and service -> provide controlled liquidity
-> learn and replicate
```

Its primary commercial objective is to find and convert the right capital.
Tokenization is one optional ownership and distribution mechanism.

## Non-Negotiable Decisions

1. Capital Discovery and Formation Intelligence is the primary commercial
   engine.
2. Mandate acceptance starts confidential pre-market immediately.
3. No deal goes live cold. Default 30-day admission requires at least 25%
   qualified soft-circle coverage; shorter windows ordinarily require more.
4. Live means close-ready and must reach a declared terminal outcome inside
   7, 14, or 30 days.
5. Bankability is a configurable dependency graph across rights, LOIs, MOUs,
   offtake, insurance, guarantees, equipment, infrastructure, incentives,
   authorities, and capital.
6. Government, regulator, agency, chamber, community, and institutional signals
   are precisely scoped and independently verifiable.
7. Independent professional conclusions are modular, portable, limited, and
   revocable through the Assurance Passport.
8. AI performs production work while accountable humans retain consequential
   and regulated authority.
9. GTCX-native systems form the product core; external parties supply adopted
   authority, professional accountability, proprietary data, and execution
   rails through typed adapters.
10. Post-close servicing, monitoring, liquidity, workout, and outcomes remain
    part of the product.
11. Every listing must use the applicable `gtcx-os/protocols` verification and
    execution rails under a signed Protocol Applicability Manifest.

## Initial Programme Phases

### P0 — Canonical contracts and fixtures

- Define the three canonical graph contracts and all demand, support, authority,
  commitment, close, and outcome taxonomies.
- Define Passport, Transaction Certainty, and Authority-Trace Receipt schemas.
- Define Transaction Jurisdiction and Execution Profile, ecosystem capability
  registry, and integration conformance contracts.
- Define Protocol Applicability Manifest and mandatory TradePass, GeoTag, GCI,
  VaultMark, PvP, PANX, and protocol-receipt listing gates.
- Map current capabilities, gaps, owners, external authorities, and Class S
  gates.
- Build Ghana, ZWCMP, Kasai, Sierra Leone diamond, Ghana gold, and Zimbabwe
  lithium fixtures.

### P1 — Capital formation and readiness

- Deliver the Capital Formation Strategy Desk, Capital Graph, investor research,
  mandate intelligence, and structure-market co-optimization.
- Deliver Opportunity, Evidence, Asset, Authority, and Bankability Rooms.
- Start confidential pre-market on mandate acceptance and maintain the governed
  demand book.
- Deliver Two-Sided Deal Teams, document factory, data room, media room, and
  secure collaboration.

### P2 — Assured time-boxed close

- Deliver Institutional Assurance Network and Government Authority Gateway.
- Deliver Transaction Certainty Record, live-admission policy, no-cold-listing
  gate, and 7/14/30-day market clock.
- Complete one bounded transaction through commitment, settlement, ownership,
  signed close pack, and live cross-ecosystem authority traces.

### P3 — Operate, provide liquidity, and learn

- Deliver servicing, covenant, support-conversion, authority, and exception
  monitoring.
- Deliver controlled secondary liquidity, refinancing, workout, redemption,
  and exit.
- Feed projected-versus-realized outcomes into Capital and Bankability Graphs.

## First End-to-End Proof

Select one **Assured and Authority-Supported Deal Programme**. It must show:

1. source-backed investor market map and approved Capital Formation Strategy;
2. complete bankability configuration with support dependencies and Plans
   A/B/C;
3. mandatory authority and independent assurance signals;
4. evidence-backed qualified soft-circle coverage before live admission;
5. 14- or 30-day live close to a declared terminal standard;
6. reconciled cash, ownership, obligations, and signed authority receipts; and
7. post-close monitoring plus credible liquidity or exit path.
