## 1. Purpose

Define the authority, responsibilities, composition, and operating procedures of the Board of Directors of GTCX.

## 2. Board Authority

The Board retains authority over:

1. **Strategy approval** — 3-year strategic plan and annual budgets.
2. **Major capital allocation** — Investments > $100K; acquisitions; divestitures.
3. **Risk appetite** — Approval of risk register and risk tolerance thresholds.
4. **Executive appointments** — CEO, CFO, CISO, and direct reports.
5. **Policy approval** — Ethics policy, security policy, data retention policy.
6. **External audit** — Appointment and remuneration of external auditors.
7. **Regulatory compliance** — Sign-off on SOC 2, FICA, POPIA compliance status.

### 3.2 Independence

At least one board member must be independent of management and free from any business relationship that could compromise impartiality.

### 3.3 Diversity

The Board commits to:

- At least one female director by 2027.
- At least one director with African financial sector experience.
- Skills matrix covering: technology, security, finance, law, and African markets.

## 6. Director Responsibilities

Each director must:

1. Act in good faith and in the best interests of GTCX.
2. Disclose conflicts of interest before related discussions.
3. Maintain confidentiality of board deliberations.
4. Complete annual governance training.
5. Review board papers at least 48 hours before meetings.

## 7. Performance Evaluation

The Board conducts an annual self-evaluation covering:

- Effectiveness of board meetings and decision-making.
- Committee performance.
- Individual director contributions.
- Skills gap analysis.

First evaluation: Target 2026-Q4.
